RoPE: How Transformers Know Word Order

Rotary Positional Embeddings encode word position by rotating vectors. The angle between two words encodes their distance -- not their absolute position.

Key insight: RoPE doesn't care about absolute position. It only cares about DISTANCE between words. "cat...mat" at positions (1, 5) gives the same attention as at positions (101, 105). The distance is 4 either way -- same rotation angle between them.

Absolute Embeddings (original BERT)

Position 513 = CRASH. The model has never seen it during training.
Each position gets a fixed, learned vector. No position 513 in the table? No embedding. Output is garbage or an error.
Hard limit: 512 tokens. Period.

RoPE (Modern Transformers)

Position 513 = just another rotation. Works fine.
The formula angle = position x theta works for any number. Nothing to look up, nothing to crash on.
With high base theta, handles 8,192+ tokens smoothly.
